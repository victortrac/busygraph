## Design Context

### Users
BusyGraph is used by the individual who chooses to run it on their own computers. The typical user is self-directed and data-driven, wants to understand how much computing they are doing over time, and values accurate, information-dense visual feedback over lightweight summaries.

### Brand Personality
Analytical, technical, beautiful.

The interface should feel precise and trustworthy first, with beauty coming from clarity, rhythm, and disciplined presentation rather than decorative effects.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a research-notebook style for the dashboard: quiet, dense, and instrument-like. Charts should remain the center of attention, supported by restrained UI chrome, strong hierarchy, and compact but readable spacing. Support both light and dark themes as first-class experiences.

Avoid generic AI-dashboard cues such as purple-blue gradients, glassmorphism, decorative hero metrics, and interchangeable white-card grids. Favor neutral surfaces, semantic accents by data type, and visual consistency between the full dashboard and the quick-stats window.

### Design Principles
1. Data first: prioritize accuracy, scale readability, and information density over ornamental UI.
2. Beauty through precision: make the interface attractive through typography, spacing, contrast, and alignment rather than effects.
3. One system, two surfaces: the dashboard and mini window should feel like the same product at different scales.
4. Dual-theme by design: light and dark modes should both be intentional, not inverted afterthoughts.
5. Calm technical tone: use restrained accents and compact layouts that support focused analysis.
